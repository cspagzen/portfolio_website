Stop Managing Backlogs, Start Managing Portfolios

Published on September 11, 2025

Backlogs are comfortable. They make work look organized. But backlogs often reward volume over value. Tickets move, charts go up and to the right, and yet the business doesn’t feel the impact. Portfolios change the unit of conversation from tasks to outcomes.

What goes wrong with backlog-first thinking

  • Activity masquerades as progress. Velocity rises while goals don’t.
  • Pet projects sneak in. Without explicit linkage to strategy, anything can look important.
  • Carryover becomes normal. Work rolls forward without re-qualification.

What a portfolio gives you instead

  • Outcome-first planning. Start with objectives, then opportunities, then initiatives.
  • Capacity truth. See where time actually goes and move it with intent.
  • Transparent tradeoffs. If everything is priority one, nothing is.

Stories still matter — in the right place

User stories are great for execution. Keep them. But let the portfolio set direction and guardrails, then let teams decide how to deliver within those boundaries.

If your organization is drowning in tickets, try this: freeze the backlog for a week, stand up a simple portfolio view, tie active work to outcomes, and ask what should stop. You’ll surface the truth fast — and you’ll make space for the work that actually moves the needle.
